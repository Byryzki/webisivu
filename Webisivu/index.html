Network attached storage with Raspberry Pi
After finding my cloud storage reaching it's limit, neuron activated in my brain. I searched my old Raspberry Pi 3B from a drawer and bought some flash drives. Prices of cloud storages are annoyingly high, so I decided to try to host my files on-prem. At first I choose the LAMP stack as the bases for Nextcloud and then switched to Nginx for better optimization. After couple of kernel panics this plan turned out working flawlessly until I found out that Nextcloud can support only one driver at the same time. I deployed VPN so I could connect to the device from anywhere and NFS to manage files from windows' directory. The project took a halt when my roommate disconnected power to the Raspberry Pi while vacuuming corrupting my entire drive.
